Running Hermes in the Background

One of the most powerful features of Hermes is its ability to run tasks in the background without interrupting your current conversation. This allows you to have multiple thought processes running simultaneously.

1. Background Tasks with Slash Commands

You can start a background task by using the /background command followed by your request. This tells Hermes to process the request in the background while you continue with your current conversation.

Example: /background Research the latest advancements in quantum computing

2. Setting Up Cron Jobs

Hermes allows you to schedule recurring tasks using its cron job functionality. This is perfect for daily briefings, reminders, or periodic data collection.

Example: Set up a daily morning brief at 8:00 AM that includes weather, motivational quotes, and your schedule.

3. The Dreaming Feature

Perhaps one of the most fascinating features is Hermes' ability to "dream" about your goals and projects. During scheduled downtime (like early morning), Hermes can analyze all your conversations, goals, and data to provide insights and recommendations.

Example: At 6:00 AM, Hermes reviews your goals, recent conversations, and projects to generate three actionable recommendations for the day plus one non-negotiable task.

4. Practical Applications

  • Research: Have Hermes research a topic in the background while you work on other tasks.
  • Content Creation: Let Hermes outline articles, write scripts, or brainstorm ideas while you focus on execution.
  • Planning: Use background tasks to develop project plans, itineraries, or strategies.
  • Learning: Have Hermes explain complex concepts in the background while you do other work.

5. Best Practices

  • Use background tasks for time-consuming operations that don't require immediate feedback.
  • Set clear, specific goals for your background tasks.
  • Combine background tasks with cron jobs for automated recurring processes.
  • Use the dreaming feature to get unexpected insights and connections.
  • Monitor your background tasks to ensure they're completing successfully.
